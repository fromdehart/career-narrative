# Idea — career-narrative

Received: Wed Apr 29 12:47:16 PM EDT 2026

AwesomeWork: Experience Blueprint

1. The Core Idea (how you describe it in one breath)

AwesomeWork turns your career into a structured, verifiable story that both humans and AI agents can understand and trust.

Not a resume.
Not a profile.
A career narrative with proof.

⸻

👤 Job Seeker Experience

Phase 1: “Tell your story” (the hook)

This is where you win or lose.

Instead of forms, the entry point is:

“Let’s walk through your career like an interview.”

Flow:

Upload resume (optional but speeds things up)
Choose focus:
“Full career”
“Last 5 years”
“Specific role or project”

Then:

🎙️ Guided audio interview

Feels like a real interviewer, not a chatbot
Dynamic follow-ups:
“What was the actual impact?”
“How do you know it worked?”
“What was hardest about that?”
“What would your manager say?”

Important:
It should occasionally challenge the user. That’s what creates signal.

⸻

Phase 2: “From story → structure”

After the interview:

The system produces a draft:

Your Career Narrative (human-readable)

Story-driven
Organized by roles/projects
Reads like a thoughtful case study, not bullets

Underneath: Structured layer (agent-readable)

Roles
Projects
Claims (what you say you did)
Outcomes (metrics, impact)
Skills inferred
Confidence levels

⸻

Phase 3: “Prove it” (this is your edge)

Now the system says:

“Let’s strengthen your story.”

For each major claim:

“You said you scaled from 0 to 1 — can you support that?”

Options:

Upload artifact (deck, doc, link)
Add context (“can’t share publicly, but…”)
Skip (explicitly allowed)

This creates:

Evidence objects
Even “light evidence” is better than none

⸻

Phase 4: References (high leverage, high risk)

Framing matters a lot here.

“Add people who can speak to your work. We’ll handle outreach.”

Flow:

User adds references
Chooses:
which experiences they relate to
whether to contact now or later

Then:

📞 AI-led reference conversation

Short, structured
Focused on:
strengths
context
credibility of claims

Output:

summarized reference insights
attached to specific roles/projects

Key:
Make this feel respectful and controlled, not invasive.

⸻

Phase 5: The Output (what the user gets)

Not a resume. Not a LinkedIn page.

They get:

1. Career Story Page

Narrative + highlights
Selected evidence
Reference snippets

2. “Agent-ready profile”

Structured, queryable
Designed for machine consumption

3. Portable identity

Shareable link
Or private, agent-only access

⸻

🤖 Agent / Recruiter Experience

This is where you differentiate hard.

Entry point:

Not browsing. Not filtering.

“What are you looking for?”

Example queries:

“PM who took a product from 0 to revenue”
“Engineer who improved system performance under scale”
“Someone strong in stakeholder alignment in messy orgs”

⸻

What they get back

Not a list of resumes.

They get:

Candidate summaries like:

Narrative snapshot
Key claims
Evidence attached
Reference signals

Example:

“Led 0→1 AI product generating $12M ARR”

Evidence: product doc, launch metrics
Reference: “strong executor in ambiguity” (former manager)

⸻

Drill-down capabilities

Agents can:

Ask follow-ups:
“Show me evidence of scale”
“Compare top 3 candidates”
Pull raw answers:
interview transcripts
Filter by:
confidence
evidence strength
reference backing

⸻

The “aha moment”

This is what you’re aiming for:

“I trust this more than a resume.”

If you don’t hit that, it doesn’t work.

⸻

⚖️ Guardrails (you need these baked into the story)

You’re stepping into sensitive territory. So explicitly position:

1. Consent-first references

user-initiated only
clear opt-in
transparent usage

2. Evidence transparency

what’s verified vs self-reported
no pretending everything is truth

3. Candidate control

what’s public vs private
ability to remove/edit
